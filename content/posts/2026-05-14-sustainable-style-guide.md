Building a sustainable wardrobe doesn't mean sacrificing style. It's about being intentional with your choices and understanding that quality often trumps quantity.

## Start with a Capsule Foundation

A well-curated capsule wardrobe forms the backbone of sustainable dressing. Invest in versatile pieces in neutral tones — a well-fitted blazer, quality denim, classic white shirts, and a little black dress. These pieces should last for years, not months.

## Know Your Fabrics

Understanding materials is key to making sustainable choices. Look for organic cotton, linen, hemp, Tencel, and recycled fibers. Avoid virgin polyester and other petroleum-based fabrics when possible. Check care labels — proper maintenance extends garment life significantly.

## The Art of Repair and Alteration

Before throwing away a garment, consider whether it can be repaired or altered. A simple stitch, a replaced button, or a trip to a tailor can give your favorite pieces a second life.

## Shop Second-Hand First

Vintage and thrift shopping is not just a trend — it's one of the most sustainable ways to dress. Unique finds, lower environmental impact, and often better quality from older garment construction.

## Support Transparent Brands

Look for brands that publish sustainability reports, use certified materials, and are transparent about their supply chain. The more information a brand shares, the more accountable they are.

---

*Sustainable fashion is a journey, not a destination. Start where you are, and make better choices one at a time.*
